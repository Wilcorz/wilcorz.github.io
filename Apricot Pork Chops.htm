Apricot-Maple Glazed Pork Chops
By Chef Charley Rozen

Pork chops have fallen out of favor as people opted for leaner meats, but, don't be discouraged.  Pork producers have trimmed down their pigs and pork is slowly making a comeback.

That's good news. After all, pork is tasty, and relatively easy to prepare.  Dressed, pork chops can make a fabulous dinner. 

This recipe will produce one of the most flavorful pork chops you'll ever eat. Apricot nectar and maple syrup complement the sauce. Pair with steamed white rice, and green beans and you have a dinner for the most discerning guest..

Ingredients:

2

 

tablespoons butter

4

 

(1 lb.) tablespoons 1/2-inch-thick pork chops

1/4

 

cup diced shallots

1/2

 

cup Apricot nectar - use only whole juice

1/4

 

cup sherry wine or dry white wine

1/4

 

cup maple syrup

1/2

 

teaspoon  Instant Chicken Bouillon

4

 

dried apricot halves, thinly sliced (optional)

1

 

tablespoon finely chopped parsley (optional)

Preparation:

MELT butter in large skillet. Add pork; cook, turning once, until no longer pink in centre. Transfer to serving plate; keep warm.

ADD shallots to skillet; cook, stirring frequently, until tender. Stir in nectar, sherry, maple syrup and bouillon. Bring to a boil; cook, stirring frequently, until sauce has thickened. Remove from heat; pour sauce over pork. Sprinkle with apricots and parsley.

Servings: 4
