*****Truffles *****

 

Chocolate Occasions

Introduction

 

Named after the rare European Fungus (Tuber melanasporum), the imported truffles resemble their namesake in costliness, unassuming appearance and snob appeal!

 

The most important ingredient in truffle-making is chocolate. It can also be the most temperamental and difficult to use. HUMIFITY is chocolate’s (and your) worst enemy.

 

There are two types of chocolate you will be using. Real chocolate, which is used for baking, and Confectionery Coating, which is used for dipping and molding truffles.

 

MAKING GANACHE

 

The truffle center is called Ganache, and is very easy to make. It is made with either Marquis (milk chocolate), Yucatan (semi-sweet chocolate), or Ivory (White chocolate). Also, added to these are whipping cream, and flavorings (flavor oils or liqueurs).

 

Flavor oils work best in Ganache as they are oil based and blend well with chocolate. If you choose to use a liqueur, the flavor will disappear in a shorter time span, as it tends to evaporate more quickly.

 

COATING CHOCOLATE

 

NOTE: Add Paramont crystals for coating glaze.

 

Chocolate used for dipping is a highly refined and specialized product with higher butterfat content than baking chocolate. Your coating chocolate may be melted in one of several ways. The microwave is the quietest but also the most dangerous, as it is easy to burn if you are unfamiliar with your microwave. Test it by using a normal setting for about 30 seconds, and then stir the coating. Put it in again for another 30 seconds, and repeat the process, checking and stirring after each try, until the chocolate is completely melted.

 

Another way to melt your coating is with an electric skillet with about 1/2 inch water in the bottom, and put the setting on WARM. Put your chocolate in a flat bottom bowl and set in the water. Wait 5 minutes and stir until melted.  NEVER drip any water into your chocolate or it will become hard and unmanageable.

 

STORAGE OF CHOCOLATE

 

Chocolate is a very stable product and has excellent keeping properties under the proper conditions. Ideal storage is characterized by the absence of light, moisture, heat and humidity.  Chocolate is most temperature-sensitive foodstuff. Coco starts to melt at 80 degrees.

 

DIPPING

 

Dipping should be done in a cool room with low humidity. Before dipping, line your working area with parchment paper. parchment paper will also be used for turning out your truffles.

 

MOLDING TRUFFLES

 

Truffles are elegant when made from molds. Fill each cavity of the mold with a little chocolate, coating side well and freeze about 1 to 2 minutes until hard. Fill cavity with the desired Ganache, freeze again for another 2 minutes, and cap the top with chocolate to seal. Again place in freezer till hard (About 2 minutes). Then pop the molded truffle out onto a piece of towel.

 

GARNISHING TRUFFLES

 

Garnishing helps to differentiate among the flavors. There are many ways to garnish your truffles. Threading involves drizzling contrasting colors of chocolate over the tops of your truffles, or sprinkling with nuts or candies, citrus rinds, coconut, cocoa or placing a delicate lay-on on top.

RECIPES

 

BASIC RECIPE

 

3/4 cup whipping cream

1 pound REAL chocolate (Marquis, Yucatan, or Ivory)

 

1) In a medium saucepan over the stove, bring cream to a boil.

2) Remove from heat, and stir in chocolate..  Let it sit about 1 minute then beat till smooth.

3) Add flavoring according to appropriate recipe.

4) Cover and refrigerate overnight.

 

ROLLING & DIPPING

 

1) Return Ganache to room temperature.

2) Form into balls of desired size (Coat hands with powdered sugar before rolling).

3) Melt coating chocolate for dipping and dip each ball of Ganache, shaking off excess chocolate before turning out onto parchment paper.

4) Garnish as desired.

 

FLAVOR IDEAS – BASIC RECIPE

 

1) Crème de Menthe - Use Marquis or Yucatan. 1 dram Cream de Menthe oil.

 

2) Cherry – Black Walnut - Use Yucatan.  Add 8 drops cherry oil, and 10-15 drops black walnut
oil. Add ¼ cup chopped cherries and ¼ cup chopped walnuts.

 

3) Bavarian Mint - Use Yucatan. 1 dram Pepperment Oil.

 

4) Cappuccino - Use Marquis or Ivory.  1 dram Coffee oil and 1 tsp Cinnamon.

 

5) Kahlua - Use Yucatan. 1 dram Kahlua Oil.

 

6) Amaretto - Use Marquis. 1 dram Amaretto oil.

 

CRUNCHES & OTHER ADDITIONS

 

 

1) Praline Crunch - Use Marquis. 1 dram Praline Oil. - Add 1 bag Brickle.

 

2) Malted Milk - Use Yucatan. - Add 1 bag malt.

 

3) Peanut butter Melt away - Use Marquis. - Add 3/4 cup peanut butter.

 

4) Coconut Crunch - Use Yucatan or Marquis. - Add 10 drops coconut oil.  Add 1 bag of toasted coconut.

 

5) Banana Crème - Use Marquis or Ivory. - Add 10-15 drops banana crème oil.  Add any

of the following: dried banana chips, chopped cherries, etc.

 

6) Raspberry - Use Yucatan. - 1 dram Raspberry oil and ¼ cup raspberry preserves.

 

7) Apricot - Use Yucatan. - 1 dram Apricot oil and ¼ cup Apricot preserves or chopped dried Apricots.

 

MORE WORK BUT WORTH THE EFFORT

 

CHOCOLATE SMOOTHIE

 

1 lb. Marquis

1/2 cup Whipping Cream

1 tsp. Butter

1 Jar Marshmallow Crème (7 oz)

1/2 tsp. Vanilla

 

Dip in white chocolate and roll in crushed walnuts.

 

CHOCOLATE CREAM CHEESE

 

10 oz. Cream Cheese to room temperature

2/3 cup Clover Honey

1 lb. Yucatan

 

Melt Honey & Chocolate together. Add cream cheese and beat until mixed.  Dip in dark and thread in white.

 

PISTACHIO-BUTTER

 

1 lb. Ivory

1/2 cup plus 4 Tblspn Unsalted Sweet butter

2 Egg yolks

3 Tblspn Water

3/4 cup finely chopped Pistachio nuts.

 

Melt chocolate, butter and water in saucepan.  Stir till smooth.  Beat in egg yolks till fluffy.  Roll balls in crushed Pistachio nuts.
