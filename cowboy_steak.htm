Cowboy Steak

When it’s time to eat steak, a bone-in rib-eye is the cut to buy. If that’s too hard to come by, a boneless rib-eye will work just fine.

Makes: 4 steaks

Total time: 25 minutes

4 bone-in beef rib-eye steaks (12 oz. each)
1 Tbsp. ground coffee
1 Tbsp. kosher salt
2 tsp. black pepper
1 tsp. each chili powder, garlic powder, dried thyme, and sugar

Nutrition Information
Per steak: 319 cal; 14g total fat (5g sat); 141mg chol; 333mg sodium; 0g total carbs (0g sugars); 0g fiber; 45g protein

 

 

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Brush grill rack with oil.

 

 

Trim steaks of excess fat and scrape bones of connective tissue.

 

 

Combine coffee, salt, pepper, chili powder, garlic powder, thyme, and sugar for the rub in a small bowl. Apply rub to both sides of steaks. Grill steaks, uncovered, over direct heat to desired doneness, about 4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Let steaks rest 5 minutes before serving.

Steps to Success

 

1. “French” the bones of the steaks by using the back of a small knife to scrape them clean.

 

2. Press about 1 teaspoon of the rub mixture onto each side of the steaks.

 

3. For attractive crosshatch marks, rotate the steaks 90 degrees halfway through cooking on one side.
