Raspberry Kuchen - 9"
Kuchen, pronounced KOO-ken, is the German word for cake.
IngredientsInstructions
CRUST:
Nonstick Coating Spray
1 Cup fresh or frozen Raspberries
1 Cup All Purpose Flour
1/2 Cup Sugar
1 Teaspoon Baking Powder
1/4 Cup Margarine or Butter, melted
1/4 Cup refrigerated Egg Product
1 Teaspoon Vanilla

CUSTARD:
1 8-ounce container Plain Yogurt
1/2 Cup Sugar
1/4 Cup refrigerated Egg Product
2 Tablespoons All-purpose Flour
1 1/2 Teaspoons finely shredded Lemon Peel
1 Teaspoon Vanilla

Optional:

Add 1/2 Cup chopped nuts to the batter for the Crust or the Custard. (really gives a different flavor).

Substitute Raspberries by using Blueberries with slices of Strawberries over the top of them and then pour Custard over.

Preheat oven to 350

Spray a 9-inch spring form pan with nonstick coating; set aside.

CRUST: Combine the 1 cup flour, 1/2 cup sugar, and 1 teaspoon baking powder in a medium mixing bowl.  Whisk the dry ingredients together. Add melted butter, 1/4 cup egg product, and 1 teaspoon vanilla.  Stir until well-mixed. Spread onto bottom of prepared pan; sprinkle with raspberries

CUSTARD: Combine 1/2 sugar, 2 tablespoons flour and lemon peel in a medium mixing bowl.  Whisk the dry ingredients together. Add yogurt, 1/4 cup egg product and 1 teaspoon vanilla. Mix till smooth; pour over berries.

Bake in a 350 oven about 45 minutes or until the center appears set when shaken gently.  Cool pan on a wire rack for 15 minutes.  Loosen and remove sides of pan.  Cool completely.  Cover; chill for two to 24 hours before serving.
