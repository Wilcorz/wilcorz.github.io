Roasted Yellow Beet Salad w/ Warm Maple-Mustard Dressing
Source: Natural Health magazine
Ingredients Instructions
4 small Yellow Beets
5 or 6 oz Baby Arugula or Spinach
1 cup Pecans (toasted)

Dressings:

1/4 cup stone-ground mustard
2 Tablespoons Maple Syrup







Note: Had this great salad at Linda White's home.
 		Preheat oven to 425.

Peel 4 small yellow beets, wrap them in aluminum foil and roast for an hour.
Unwrap and slice them when they stop steaming.
(I found that when I do it this way, the beets darken in places because of the foil, which isn't that visually appealing - and you know how I am about that!!!
SO, sometimes I also peel them and put them in some water and boil them until tender.)
To make the dressing, mix 1/4 cup stone-ground mustard and 2 Tablespoons of maple syrup together in a bowl.  Set aside.  When ready to serve, microwave for 30 seconds or mix and gently heat in a pan over low heat.
Peel and slice 1 small red onion, then toast 1 cup of pecans over medium heat, tossing until lightly browned (5-7 minutes).
Drizzle warm dressing over 5 or 6 ounces of baby arugula or spinach in a bowl and toss.  Top with beets, pecans, and onion, toss lightly to mix and serve.
