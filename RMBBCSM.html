Roger's version of Meatballs with Blue Cheese Sauce and Mushrooms

Ingredients

Meatballs

(I use the Costco meatballs but if you want to make your own below is a recipe )

If making your owm meatballs:

2 lbs lean ground beef
1/2 cup fine dry breadcrumb
1/2 cup milk
2 eggs
1 1/2 teaspoons salt (or part garlic salt)

Mushrooms:
1 pound Portabella Mushrooms (sliced and then cut in half)
2 Tablespoons Butter

Sauce

1/4 cup butter
1/4 cup flour
1 -2 clove garlic, mashed
1 cup half-and-half cream
1 1/2 cups chicken broth
1/2 cup crumbled blue cheese (4 ounces)
chopped parsley or chives or green onion (garnish) (optional)
pepper or paprika (garnish) (optional)

(If using Costco meatballs just place in slow cooker and heat per instructions. DO NOT add sauce until the meatballs are defrosted and heated)

If making your own Meatballs: Preheat oven to 5oo F.

Combine meat, crumbs, milk, eggs and salt in a bowl. Mix lightly to blend. Shape into balls about the size of marbles. (Makes about 100 meatballs). Arrange on shallow, rimmed baking pans and bake for 4 to 5 minutes or until lightly browned.

For Mushrooms: Heat Butter in a large pan and add mushrooms. Cook until almost all liquid is gone. Pour cooked mushrooms over meatballs (do not mix in yet). Do not wash pan as you will use it to make the following sauce.

For Sauce: Heat butter in a pan over medium heat, stir in the flour and mashed garlic and cook until blended and bubbly. ( I usually cook this until it has a nice roasted smell and is a medium brown in color) Gradually stir in the half& half and chicken broth. Cook, stirring, until thickened. (This can take about 15 minutes if you like a thick sauce) Add the crumbled blue cheese and stir until blended. Add salt if needed. (I do not add salt but do add pepper)

 
To Serve (if made ahead): Refrigerate the meatballs and sauce until time to serve. 6. Reheat meat and sauce together, covered, over low heat, stirring gently several times (can use microwave).

Sprinkle with garnish just before serving.

Keep warm in a crockpot or chafing dish.
